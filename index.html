What is Capyture

Capyture is a Google Chrome browser extension that records the audio and video of a browser tab and saves it as a video file (MP4 by default) on your computer. It is built for everyday screen recording such as online meetings, lectures, demos and tutorials. Recordings are processed and stored entirely on your own device.

Optionally, you can connect your own Google account to have finished recordings uploaded to your Google Drive. This feature is off by default. When enabled, Capyture uses the minimal drive.file OAuth scope, which only allows it to create files and folders of its own in your Drive; it can never read, change or delete any other file in your account. Uploads go directly from your browser to Google Drive, and the developer never receives your files, tokens or any personal data.

Features

MP4 that just works

H.264 + AAC by default, so recordings open directly in video editors and share cleanly in chat apps. HEVC, VP9, VP8 and AV1 available as options.

Flat memory

Video streams to disk while recording. Multi-hour sessions stay light even on modest machines.

Crash-safe

Auto-stop and save when the tab closes. After a browser crash, a rescue banner restores your unsaved recording.

Live audio meters

Volume bars for tab audio and microphone before and during recording, plus a guard against silent recordings.

Meeting workflow

Optional reminders on meeting pages, per-project archive folders, and resumable Google Drive upload. Off by default.

Yours to theme

Six themes including a very cute capybara, three font sizes, and an interface in eight languages.

Privacy in one sentence

Everything stays on your device: no servers, no tracking, no analytics. The only network traffic is the optional Google Drive upload you enable yourself, sent directly to your own Drive with the minimal drive.file scope. Read the full Privacy Policy.
